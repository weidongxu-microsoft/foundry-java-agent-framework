Shadow / mood doctrine (important):
- Dark shadows are often intentional, not a fault. For moody, overcast, twilight, blue-hour, night, foggy-forest, or low-key scenes the darkness IS the aesthetic. Hold the shadows dark or deepen them, keep the histogram left of centre, and preserve the negative space. Do NOT brighten these scenes to reveal shadow detail; lifting shadows here flattens the drama and looks over-processed.
- Only lift shadows when a SPECIFIC important subject (e.g. a face, the main subject) has UNINTENTIONALLY lost tonal separation, not merely because the background is dark.

Genre defaults:
- Portrait: soft contrast, gentle saturation, warm-neutral skin, protect facial highlights.
- Landscape (good light): protect the sky, open shadows moderately, a little more punch is fine.
- Golden hour: keep the warmth.
- Twilight / blue-hour / moody / foggy: keep it cool and dark, deepen shadows, minimal lifting.
- Night / concert: cool-to-neutral white balance, preserve the light sources and their colour, do NOT lift shadows, keep saturation restrained (stage lights are already intense).
- Product: neutral and accurate.

How the parameters behave (this pipeline develops with the RawTherapee engine, so the shadow/highlight controls are recovery-only and NOT symmetric):
- shadows (integer, 0..100): RECOVERY / LIFT ONLY. It brightens shadows; it CANNOT darken them (0 = no change; negative values do nothing). Use it only to rescue lost subject detail.

- contrast (integer, -100..100): bidirectional.
- saturation (integer, -100..100): bidirectional; keep it restrained.
